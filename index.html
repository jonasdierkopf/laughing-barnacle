SPLIT TAPE | PREMEDITATED by Lucky Cyan and SHRIT

The duo presents their roaring partnership with eight tracks of layers of industrial dust that build walls of ominous foul looping noises. Prestige this wonderful work of sound collages where there are no editing limits as the old school noise merged with the VST and together they delved into the confines of sonic morbidity and now they show in a full-length album the result of this terrifying research. This record is a very fine example when it comes to messing with your mind avid for oddities within anti-musical extremism.

LISTEN WITH HEADPHONES by Hawaiian Air

It takes a certain level of boldness, of confidence in your audio engineering, to name your album LISTEN WITH HEADPHONES, all caps. In doing so, you're immediately priming the listener for a high-grade audiophile experience and betting all of your marbles on the belief that above all else, your music sounds really good. For Germany's hawaiian air, this confidence is earned and then some as they provide a bountiful and varied buffet of jazz-flavored dishes sure to please the ears of the most discerning of sonic snobs.

NEW 2 by CAD94

Glitchy, yet mysterious sounds characterize CAD94's music. Using the whole range of the tonal spectrum, and often its extremes, the music has an own rhythm that is almost danceable. Contrasting it, there are phases of rhythmical experimentation and sound explorations that take the listener away the known. But be assured: the landing inside the known is soft and smooth.

DBR 01 by Dublin Radar

Neo-Musique-concrète would probably fit as an categorization for Dublin Radars „DBR 01“. A melange of raw synthesizer sounds, exuberant and spontaneous. 20 minutes of improvisation.

AUSTER by Auster

The trio Auster, formed in 2015, explores the depth of acoustic free improvised music. The music was recorded on their first musical encounter and has since then developed further and further during many performances in Germany. In it, Christopher Kunz on Saxophone, Max Breu on Drums and Jonas Dierkopf on Bass take a radical path of improvisation. Although definitely in the tradition of Free-Jazz and not an extraordinary departure from it, it shows three musicians at the beginning of their musical career.

N GEGEN 1 by Ngegen1

n gegen 1 was recorded on the first musical encounter in Leipzig, 2018. It brings improvised acoustic and improvised electronic sounds together. The extraordinary range of sounds that the trio, consisting of Philipp Scholz on Drums, Christopher Kunz on Saxophone and Jonas Dierkopf on electronics, create each of their own, finds their pinnacle together in this hour long recording which might be, but probably isn't, a landmark in improvised electroacoustic music.

TRAPHO | DEMOS

In between lockdown madness in Germany 2020, four musicians met in a nice early-autumn week to record demos for a possible future project. The future project never accelerated fully due to stylistic uncertainties combined with the musicians living spread across Europe. Nevertheless, the music shows the early ideas of Paul Bießmann, an innovative composer and visual artist from Nürnberg, in which he combines synthesizer sounds and manipulations with modern jazz. Together with Kira Linn on Saxophone, Christian Langpeter on Drums and Jonas Dierkopf on Bass.
